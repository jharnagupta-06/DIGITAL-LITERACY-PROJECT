## What I Did in Each Task

- **Task 1 – Digital Literacy Infographic:**  
  I created a one-page infographic using **Canva** to explain digital literacy, useful digital tools for students, and safe internet practices. This taught me how to combine **visuals and text** to make learning engaging and easy to understand.  

- **Task 2 – Student Digital Portfolio:**  
  I built my professional online presence on **GitHub, LinkedIn, and Kaggle**. On GitHub, I created a profile README showcasing my learning goals. LinkedIn helped me highlight my academic details and connect with peers. Kaggle allowed me to practice coding and data projects. This task made me realize the importance of **sharing my work online responsibly**.  

- **Task 3 – Coding & Collaboration Platforms:**  
  I practiced a beginner-level coding challenge on **HackerRank** and earned a badge. I also created a **Google Form quiz** and the link is https://forms.gle/j35CZANaK1ntkENc7 which is on the topic of digital literacy for my batchmates, linking it to a Google Sheet to track responses. These exercises improved my **problem-solving skills and collaborative abilities**.  

- **Task 4 – Professional Email & Social Media Etiquette:**  
  I wrote two professional emails—one requesting an assignment extension and another expressing internship interest. I also made a **social media Do’s and Don’ts checklist** to help students communicate responsibly online. This reinforced the importance of **clarity, politeness, and professionalism** in digital communication.  

- **Task 5 – Cybercrime Awareness Case Study & Prevention:**  
  I researched **cyberbullying** and created a case study along with a prevention checklist. I learned how **even casual online actions** can have real consequences and how to stay safe by using strong passwords, verifying information, and reporting issues to official channels like [National Cyber Crime Portal](https://cybercrime.gov.in).
