# Stay Safe Online – Prevention Checklist

## 1. Verify sender details
Always check the sender’s number, email, or social media account before clicking links or opening attachments. Attackers often pretend to be friends, banks, or institutions.

## 2. Never share OTPs or UPI PINs 
Your PIN and OTP are private. Sharing them, even if someone claims urgency, can lead to instant money theft.

## 3. Use official apps only
Download UPI, banking, or social media apps from trusted stores like Google Play or App Store. Fake apps can steal passwords, money, or personal data.

## 4. Enable two-factor authentication (2FA)
Add an extra security layer on social media, email, and financial apps. Even if your password is leaked, 2FA prevents unauthorized access.

## 5. Be careful with urgent messages or threats
Cyberbullies and fraudsters create panic to force action. Always pause, verify, and think before responding.

## 6. Monitor your accounts and online activity regularly 
Check bank statements and UPI transactions daily. Report any suspicious transactions immediately.

## 7. Use secure networks
Avoid public Wi-Fi when logging in to social media, email, or UPI apps. Public networks can be easily hacked.

## 8. Keep software and apps updated
Regular updates patch security flaws and protect your devices from malware and hackers.

## 9. Report cyberbullying or suspicious activity
Use trusted channels to report:
- **National Cyber Crime Portal:** [cybercrime.gov.in](https://cybercrime.gov.in)  
- **Helpline:** 1930

## 10. Use strong, unique passwords
Avoid easy-to-guess passwords. Combine letters, numbers, and symbols for each account.
